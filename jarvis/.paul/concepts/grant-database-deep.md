### 1.1 Utah Small Business Credit Initiative (USBCI)

**What it actually is:** This is NOT a grant — it is a loan participation program. The framing of "$3K base + $2K bonus for women/rural" refers to reduced fees or enhanced terms in some contexts, not cash grants. Clarifying this matters so you don't chase it expecting free money.

**How it works:**
- Administered by Utah Governor's Office of Economic Opportunity (GOEO) at business.utah.gov/usbci
- You do NOT apply to the state — you apply through a participating lender
- The state takes a participation position in your loan, allowing lenders to offer better terms to otherwise-marginalized borrowers
- Loan amounts: $10,000–$20,000,000
- Target: businesses owned by women and culturally/economically/geographically diverse entrepreneurs (29% of program funds earmarked)
- Washington County: YES, eligible statewide — check the enrolled lender list at business.utah.gov/usbci for participating banks in the Saint George area

**What it unlocks for Jonathan:**
- If any of his ventures need capital (build-out for Venture C, fleet for Venture D), this de-risks the loan for the lender
- The women/minority angle (LGBTQ+ connections, female co-ownership) potentially unlocks priority access
- Not a grant, but can dramatically improve loan terms — don't skip it

**Ease:** 3 (loan application process, not grant)
**Applies to:** C, D, E
**Confidence:** HIGH (official GOEO source)
**URL:** https://business.utah.gov/usbci/

### 2.2 What People Report Causes Rejections

Based on aggregated community reports:

1. **Mismatched narrative** — Most rejections happen because applicants describe WHAT they do, not the IMPACT on a specific population. Funders want to see "45 Southern Utah seniors with improved mobility metrics" not "we run a longevity program."

2. **No financial documentation prepared** — Rolling grants often reject applications that can't provide basic financials on demand. Maintain: IRS determination letter (A, B), last 2 years business tax returns (C, D, E), and a 1-page balance sheet at all times.

3. **Skipping the match question** — Many funders ask "do you have other funding?" — applicants who answer "no" look less fundable. Answer: "We are applying to multiple programs simultaneously and view this grant as part of a diversified funding approach." This is more fundable than "this is our only source."

4. **Generic mission statements** — "We promote wellness" loses to "We serve 200 Southern Utah adults over 65 with HBOT and photobiomodulation therapies, reducing hospital readmission rates among our autism patient population." Specificity wins.
